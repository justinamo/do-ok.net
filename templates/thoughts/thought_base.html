Information-based Economy

26 April 2021

One thought that has been percolating in my head for a little bit of time is in the future, the economy will have two halves: internet-based infrastructure, and everything that infrastructure facilitates. People will either be able to make their living doing either 1) supporting and expanding the infrastructure, or 2) utilizing the infrastructure. I believe the economy is trending in this direction, towards a future where most of that infrastructure is facilited by computers. Computers are a specialized enough domain where, currently, the gulf between the knowledge needed to program a computer vs. the knowledge needed to use a computer is sufficiently wide so that there is a stark contrast between computer-based forms of infrastructure and others.

There will be enough surplus in the infrastructure providers that they will be able to employ many people, each responsible for a small specific subset of that infrastructure.

Due to the speed and efficiency of transmitting information over the internet, it will eventually replace other forms of infrastructure for a particular good, especially if the good does not have some intrinsic quality that means it must be consumed in the physical realm. For goods that are able to be completely digitized, and where the demand for that good is able to be shifted completely over to the digitized version, the entire exchange of that good will happen over the internet.

For physical goods, there has to be a separate physical system alongside the internet infrastructure, namely shipping and logistics, and the size of the physical infrastructure will depend on the demand for physical goods by the consumer.
